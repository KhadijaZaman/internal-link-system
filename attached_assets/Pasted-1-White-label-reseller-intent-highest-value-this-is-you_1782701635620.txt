1. White-label / reseller intent (highest value — this is your positioning)
 These are the lowest-competition, highest-fit prompts. Almost nobody is writing content explicitly for "white-label GEO for agencies," and the person asking is dead-center ICP.
"Is there a white-label AI visibility tool I can offer my agency clients?"
"Can I resell GEO / AI search optimization under my own brand?"
"White-label GEO platform for marketing agencies"
"AI visibility tools that offer agency or reseller plans"
"I want to add AI visibility as a service without hiring — what tool lets me do that in the background?"
"Best white-label SEO tools that also cover AI search / ChatGPT visibility"
2. Comparison/evaluation (high value — BOFU)
 The agency has a shortlist and wants a verdict. You already have displacement pages built for most of these.
"Wellows vs Peec AI for agencies"
"Peec AI vs Profound vs Otterly — which is best for a small agency?"
"Profound alternatives for small agencies"
"Cheapest AI visibility tracking tool for agencies"
"Is Profound worth it for a small agency or is there something more affordable?"
3. Tool discovery (high value — MOFU)
 Solution-aware, looking for a shortlist. The phrasing is conversational, which is exactly the recommendation moment you want to be cited in.
"What are the best tools to track brand visibility across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini?"
"Best GEO tools for agencies in 2026"
"What software can I use to monitor my clients' citations in AI search?"
"What's a good AI visibility platform for a small marketing agency managing 5–15 clients?"
"Tools to optimize content so it gets cited by AI search engines"
4. Service delivery / how-to (high value — where the answer can be a tool recommendation)
 The agency is figuring out how to deliver this. If the AI answer is "here's the workflow, and a tool like Wellows handles it," you win the recommendation inside the instructions.
"How do I offer GEO as a service to my existing SEO clients?"
"How to add AI visibility as a new service line at my agency"
"How to improve a client's chances of being cited by ChatGPT"
"How do I run a GEO audit for a client?"
"How to report AI search visibility to clients"
5. Pricing / packaging (high value — high commercial intent)
 Business-model questions. The agency has decided to do it and is working out economics — very late-stage.
"How much should I charge clients for GEO / AI visibility services?"
"How to package GEO into an existing SEO retainer"
"What's a fair price for AI visibility monitoring per client?"
6. Category education (volume, softer fit — TOFU)
 The agency just got asked by a client and is orienting. Worth winning for brand, but the searcher isn't always the ICP.
"My SEO clients are asking how they can show up in ChatGPT and AI results — how do I help them?"
"What is generative engine optimization and how is it different from SEO?"
"Should my agency start offering AI visibility as a service?"
"Are AI search engines actually sending traffic to websites?"
7. Skeptical / validation (TOFU–MOFU)
 The agency owner is deciding whether this is real before committing. Winning these with a credible, data-backed answer builds trust early.
"Does GEO actually work or is it just hype?"
"Can you really influence what ChatGPT recommends?"
"Is optimizing for ChatGPT worth the effort for a small agency?"
